Metropolis-Hastings

How it works:

Metropolis-Hastings proposes new samples from a proposal distribution and accepts or rejects them based on a probability ratio. This simple but powerful method can sample from any distribution we can evaluate.

Key idea:

Even if we can't sample directly from our target distribution, we can construct a Markov chain that has the target as its equilibrium distribution.

💡 Tips & Best Practices

  • Start with default parameters and adjust one at a time to see their effect
  • Metropolis-Hastings: Too small step size = slow mixing, too large = low acceptance
  • Gibbs Sampling: Works great when conditionals are easy to compute
  • SMC: More particles = better approximation but slower
  • HMC: Use for complex distributions with gradients available
  • Watch the acceptance rate! Ideal is 20-50% for most problems
  • The burn-in period lets the chain converge before recording samples
